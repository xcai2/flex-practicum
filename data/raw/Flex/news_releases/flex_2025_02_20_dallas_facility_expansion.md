# Flex Expands U.S. Manufacturing Capacity with New Dallas Facility for AI-Driven Power Demands

**Date:** February 20, 2025  
**Type:** Facility Expansion / CapEx Investment  
**Source:** https://investors.flex.com/news/news-details/2025/Flex-Expands-U.S.-Manufacturing-Capacity-to-Meet-Customers-Growing-AI-Driven-Power-Demands-with-New-Dallas-Facility/

**Tags:** CapEx, facility expansion, Dallas, AI power infrastructure, power pods, manufacturing capacity  
**Relevance:** VERY HIGH - Major U.S. manufacturing expansion for AI data center power  
**CapEx Related:** Yes (400,000 sq ft facility investment)  
**AI Related:** Yes

---

## Summary

Flex announced the expansion of its U.S. manufacturing capacity with a new 400,000-square-foot Dallas manufacturing facility focused on power products for AI data centers. The facility significantly boosts production capacity for grid-to-chip data center power infrastructure solutions, including power pods, power distribution units, and low-voltage switchgear, enhancing Flex's ability to meet rising power infrastructure demands driven by AI adoption.

---

## Facility Details

### Dallas Manufacturing Facility

**Size and Scope:**
- **400,000 square feet** of manufacturing space
- Located in Dallas, Texas (4001 N. Prairie Creek Road, East Dallas)
- Focused on power products for data centers
- Hub for Flex's growing North American customer base

**Products Manufactured:**
- **Power Pods:** Prefabricated modular power infrastructure
- **Power Distribution Units (PDUs):** Rack-level and facility-level power distribution
- **Low-Voltage Switchgear:** Electrical distribution and protection systems
- **Grid-to-Chip Solutions:** Complete power infrastructure from utility to server

**Strategic Significance:**
- Significantly boosts U.S. production capacity
- Enhances production efficiency for power infrastructure
- Reduces production lead times for U.S. customers
- Strengthens domestic manufacturing footprint
