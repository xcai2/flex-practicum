## Full Press Release

**_Mikros Technologies' thermal management capabilities complement Jabil's existing data center solutions to enable the growth of AI infrastructure_**

ST. PETERSBURG, Fla.--(BUSINESS WIRE)-- Jabil Inc. (NYSE: JBL), a global leader in design, manufacturing, and supply chain solutions, today announced the successful acquisition of Mikros Technologies LLC, a leader in the engineering and manufacturing of liquid cooling solutions for thermal management, completed October 1.

Located in the hills of New Hampshire, Mikros Technologies has specialized in thermal management and precision jetting solutions for over 30 years.

As the continued adoption of artificial intelligence, energy storage, and electric vehicles drives higher-power density systems across consumer and commercial industries, liquid cooling has emerged as a more energy-efficient alternative to air cooling for many applications. This acquisition will provide Jabil with critical capabilities for helping our customers manage the intense thermal requirements of their current and next-generation products while keeping sustainability and cost considerations top of mind.

## Analysis Notes

This acquisition is strategically important for Jabil's AI data center strategy, providing critical thermal management capabilities that are essential for AI infrastructure. Liquid cooling is becoming increasingly necessary as AI workloads drive higher power densities.

**Strategic Rationale:**
- AI workloads generate significantly more heat than traditional compute
- Liquid cooling is 10-100x more efficient than air cooling for high-density systems
- Hyperscalers are rapidly adopting liquid cooling for AI clusters
- Thermal management is a key bottleneck for AI infrastructure deployment
- Mikros's proprietary technology provides competitive differentiation

**Market Context:**
- AI accelerators (GPUs) can consume 400-700W per chip
- AI clusters can exceed 100kW per rack (vs. 10-20kW traditional)
- Liquid cooling becoming standard for AI infrastructure
- Growing market for direct-to-chip and immersion cooling
- Sustainability and energy efficiency driving adoption

**Competitive Positioning:**
- Positions Jabil as full-stack AI infrastructure provider
- Complements power management (Hanley Energy acquisition)
- Enables end-to-end thermal and power solutions
- Differentiates from competitors without liquid cooling capabilities

**Financial Impact:**
- Acquisition price not disclosed
- Completed October 1, 2024
- Part of broader AI infrastructure investment strategy
- Synergies with $500M North Carolina facility expansion
- Integration with Hanley Energy power management capabilities
