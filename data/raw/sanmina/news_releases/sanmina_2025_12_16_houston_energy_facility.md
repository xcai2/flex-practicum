## Summary

Sanmina announced a major expansion of its Energy business with a new state-of-the-art factory in Houston, Texas focused on the U.S. energy market. The facility will manufacture medium-voltage distribution transformers, instrument transformers, and switchgear, with production expected to start in 2027. Initial customer commitments are already in place.

## Market Context

### AI Data Center Power Infrastructure

**Power Requirements:**
- Traditional data center: 5-20 MW
- AI data center: 50-200+ MW (10x higher)
- Liquid-cooled AI clusters: 100+ kW per rack
- Massive power distribution infrastructure needed

**Equipment Needs:**
- **Medium-Voltage Transformers:** Step down utility power (typically 13.8 kV or 34.5 kV) to data center distribution voltage
- **Switchgear:** Protect and distribute power to different zones
- **Backup Power:** UPS systems and generators
- **Power Distribution Units (PDUs):** Rack-level power distribution

**Market Growth:**
- Hyperscaler CapEx increasing for AI infrastructure
- Utility companies struggling to meet data center power demand
- Long lead times for power equipment (transformers, switchgear)
- Domestic manufacturing preference for critical infrastructure

### Energy Infrastructure Market

**U.S. Market Drivers:**
- Grid modernization ($100+ billion investment)
- Renewable energy integration
- Data center power demand
- Electric vehicle infrastructure
- Industrial electrification

**Supply Chain:**
- Transformer shortages and long lead times
- Domestic manufacturing preference (CHIPS Act, IRA)
- Critical infrastructure security concerns
- Tariffs on imported electrical equipment
