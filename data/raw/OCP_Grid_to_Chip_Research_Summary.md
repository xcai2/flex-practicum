# OCP and Grid to Chip Infrastructure Research Summary
## Contract Manufacturing Industry Analysis

**Research Date:** February 3, 2026  
**Companies Analyzed:** Jabil Inc., Celestica Inc., Benchmark Electronics, Sanmina Corporation, Flex Ltd.  
**Focus Areas:** Open Compute Project (OCP) involvement and Grid to Chip Infrastructure capabilities

---

## Executive Summary

This research analyzes the OCP engagement and grid-to-chip infrastructure capabilities of five major contract manufacturers in the data center industry. The findings reveal significant variation in strategic positioning, with Flex Ltd. demonstrating the most comprehensive grid-to-chip approach, while Benchmark Electronics shows limited direct OCP engagement.

## Detailed Company Analysis

### 1. Jabil Inc. (JBL)

**OCP Engagement Level:** Strong

**OCP Products:**
- J322OR ORV3 all-NVMe storage server (OCP-listed)
- J-422G servers (AMD EPYC "Turin"-based, OCP-compliant, October 2025)
- Active participation at OCP Global Summit 2025

**Grid to Chip Infrastructure:**
- **Explicit Focus:** Modular, integrated power solutions enable power from "grid to chip"
- **Power Management:** PDUs, UPS systems, low/medium-voltage switchgear
- **Liquid Cooling:** Acquired Mikros Technologies (October 2024) for microchannel cold plates
- **Rack Integration:** Complete rack-level systems including compute, storage, switching, photonics, power, and cooling

**Strategic Investments:**
- $500 million U.S. investment in cloud and AI data center infrastructure (June 2025)
- Hanley Energy Group acquisition for AI data center power management (January 2026)
- Endeavour partnership for 2 gigawatts/year elastic AI infrastructure capacity (July 2025)

**Growth Metrics:**
- Infrastructure segment: 40%+ year-over-year growth (July 2025)
- Now accounts for 44% of total revenue

**Key Differentiator:** Comprehensive acquisitions strategy (Mikros, Hanley Energy) strengthens vertical integration from grid to chip.

### 2. Celestica Inc. (CLS)

**OCP Engagement Level:** Strong

**OCP Products:**
- DS1000: 1U 48-port 1GbE switch (OCP Accepted, first ECS Sub-Project contributor)
- DS5000: 64x800G AI/ML switch with RunBMC specification
- DS6000/DS6001: 1.6TbE switches (air-cooled and hybrid cooling, October 2025)
- ES1500: 2.5GbE PoE access switch
- EG1050: Edge gateway with WiFi and 5G (OCP Inspired)

**Grid to Chip Infrastructure:**
- **Power Management:** Advanced custom power designs with intelligent energy storage, telemetry, and redundancy
- **Thermal Solutions:** Single-phase and dual-phase liquid cooling, in-row and in-rack CDUs
- **Rack Integration:** Turn-key solutions with Rack Configurator Tool, regionalized global footprint

**Strategic Position:**
- Market share leader in 800G high-speed interconnects
- Transformed into design-centric platform integrator
- CCS segment: 31% revenue growth in 2026
- AI networking demand: 150%+ surge

**Key Products:**
- SD6300: Ultra-dense 4U storage with 108 drive bays (November 2025)
- SC6100: Next-gen 2U all-flash storage controller with PCIe Gen5 SSDs

**Key Differentiator:** Strongest networking portfolio with market leadership in 800G interconnects and comprehensive OCP switch family.

### 3. Benchmark Electronics (BHE)

**OCP Engagement Level:** Limited Direct Engagement

**OCP Connection:**
- Partnership with Oxide Computer for OCP-inspired server racks
- YouTube presentation: "HPC/ML Benchmarks and Extensions for OCP Servers" (3 years ago)
- No OCP-accepted products identified

**Grid to Chip Infrastructure:**
- **Focus Area:** High-performance computing and supercomputing (not hyperscale data centers)
- **Liquid Cooling:** Proven experience with fully operational U.S.-based liquid cooling test and manufacturing
- **HPC Heritage:** Multiple systems on Top 500 supercomputer list, Intel Aurora Exascale Supercomputer

**Advanced Capabilities:**
- Advanced thermal management modeling
- Precision machining and metal joining for blade casing and fluid channels
- Functional-circuit testing under actual liquid-cooled conditions
- Novel cryogenic materials for quantum computing

**Manufacturing Expansion:**
- Guadalajara facility: 50% expansion in regional footprint (June 2025)
- Strong U.S.-based manufacturing presence (Huntsville, Alabama)

**Key Differentiator:** Specialization in HPC/supercomputing with government and research sector focus, less emphasis on hyperscale data center infrastructure.

**Notable Gap:** Limited public information on comprehensive power delivery systems and rack-level power infrastructure compared to competitors.

### 4. Sanmina Corporation (SANM)

**OCP Engagement Level:** Strong (Platinum Member)

**OCP Products:**
- ORv3 Rack: Fully welded frame, 1,400 kg load rating, DC busbar (48V DC up to 18 kW)
- ORv3 Accessories: Door kit, modular side panels, IT gear rails
- Liquid-cooled solutions: Manifolds, heat exchangers, CDUs
- Listed in OCP Solution Providers Directory and AI Marketplace

**Grid to Chip Infrastructure:**
- **Major Acquisition:** ZT Systems data center infrastructure manufacturing business from AMD (completed October 2025)
- **Energy Infrastructure:** Houston transformer manufacturing facility (late 2025) for distribution transformers
- **Rack Integration:** BTO/CTO services, RACK IQ product line for heavy-duty IT racks
- **Vertical Integration:** Backplanes, power sub-assemblies, cooling sub-assemblies, PCBs, servers, racks

**Growth Metrics:**
- Q1 FY2026: 59% year-over-year revenue increase
- Driven by AI infrastructure demand

**Subsidiaries:**
- Viking Enterprise Solutions: Servers and storage for datacenters
- Viking Technology: Memory modules and SSDs for enterprise applications

**Key Differentiator:** ZT Systems acquisition significantly strengthens hyperscale capabilities; Houston transformer facility positions for complete grid-to-chip solutions.

**Notable Gap:** While ORv3 rack includes DC busbar, detailed information on comprehensive power delivery systems is limited compared to Flex and Jabil.

### 5. Flex Ltd. (FLEX)

**OCP Engagement Level:** Very Strong (Platinum Member)

**OCP Products:**
- Flex Open Rack V3 (ORv3): Next-generation OCP platform
- Flex Modular Compute Platform: OCP DC-MHS 2.0 compliant with Intel processor support
- Flex Secure Control Module (DC-SCM 2.0): Server management and security

**Grid to Chip Infrastructure:**

**Most Comprehensive Approach:**
- **Explicit "Grid to Chip" Strategy:** Dedicated resources and messaging on grid-to-chip optimization
- **New Power Architecture:** Traditional 3-phase 415/480V AC → Proposed ±400V DC / 800 VDC
- **Dallas Manufacturing Plant:** Opened October 2025 specifically for grid-to-chip data center power solutions

**AI Infrastructure Platform (October 2025 Launch):**
- Industry's first globally manufactured, fully integrated platform
- Integrates power, cooling, and compute into pre-engineered, modular designs
- **Deployment Speed:** 30% faster deployment, reduces timeline from 12+ months to 6-12 months
- **Capacity:** Pre-engineered systems for 1+ MW AI blocks

**Breakthrough Products:**
- **1MW Racks:** High-density, liquid-cooled IT and OCP-inspired power racks supporting +/-400V and 800 VDC
- **CESS (Capacitive Energy Storage System):** First-to-market UL 1973-certified, reduces electrical disturbances from AI workloads
- **Modular CDU:** Scalable up to 1.8 MW flexible capacity
- **Prefabricated Power Pods and Skids:** Factory-assembled, modular systems

**Power Solutions Portfolio:**
- Grid-to-rack critical power (medium/low voltage AC/DC conversion)
- 1 MW+ DC PDUs
- In-rack power solutions (33kW power shelves with 6 PSUs)
- Chip-level power management with Vertical Power Delivery (VPD)
- VPD supports 1000A+ with ultra-fast load transient response

**Liquid Cooling:**
- JetCool (Flex company): Microjet technology for precision chip-level cooling
- Direct-to-chip liquid cooling
- Advanced CDUs, chillers, heat rejection systems

**Flex Subsidiaries:**
- **Flex Power Modules:** DC/DC converters up to 3kW
- **Anord Mardix:** Switchgear, busway, power distribution
- **Crown Technical Systems:** Turn-key power solutions
- **JetCool:** Liquid cooling with microjet technology

**Global Scale:**
- Manufacturing facilities in 30 countries
- Regional deployment capabilities
- Resilient supply chains with Flex Pulse® tools

**Key Differentiator:** Most comprehensive and explicit grid-to-chip approach with industry-first integrated AI infrastructure platform. Strongest vertical integration from grid-level (Anord Mardix) to chip-level (VPD, JetCool).

## Conclusion

The contract manufacturing industry for AI data center infrastructure shows clear differentiation in strategic approaches. Flex Ltd. emerges as the leader in comprehensive grid-to-chip solutions with its industry-first AI Infrastructure Platform, while Jabil Inc. pursues aggressive growth through strategic acquisitions. Celestica Inc. dominates networking with 800G market leadership, Sanmina Corporation shows strongest revenue growth with ZT Systems integration, and Benchmark Electronics maintains focus on HPC/supercomputing rather than hyperscale data centers.

For Flex Inc. competitive intelligence, the key insight is that grid-to-chip integration, OCP ecosystem engagement, and prefabricated modular solutions represent critical differentiators in the rapidly growing AI data center market. The companies investing most heavily in these areas (Flex Ltd., Jabil Inc., Sanmina Corporation) are capturing the highest growth rates and market positioning.
